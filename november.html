Greetings Friends: Welcome to fall — such a beautiful time of year!

As outlined in our first newsletter, we wanted to provide additional details about the hopes and goals of our new ministries. These ministries were designed to help us work together, using the efforts outlined in our Vision Statements, to become the church we believe God yearns for us to be.

The first ministry we will consider is a new one — our Stewardship Ministry.

Understanding Stewardship

When we talk about stewardship, we want everyone to share a common understanding of how this ministry nurtures our life together as a church family.

As we gathered as a new team, we reached out to our National Church, as they have a department that focuses on stewardship. We spoke with Karen Plater, Associate Secretary of Stewardship at The Presbyterian Church in Canada. After our conversation, Karen asked if we would consider joining her in attending the annual Stewardship Conference offered by the Presbyterian Church (USA). She said this was an excellent conference and she liked to take a group of people from congregations across Canada to attend each year. She offered to pay our expenses to participate — an opportunity which felt like a God-given gift.

We also heard messages of generosity, like the words the apostle Paul spoke in 2 Corinthians 9:7.

New Orleans Stewardship Conference

This year, the conference was held in New Orleans, Louisiana, and it became clear that it was no coincidence as to why. It was exactly 20 years ago that New Orleans was devastated by the powerful Hurricane Katrina.

During the week, we heard many stories of churches facing similar challenges that St. Paul‚Äôs faces today. Some were large churches, while others quite small. Yet, we were inspired by stories of rebirth and renewal, and the realization that God was with them through it all.

One pastor shared her story of leading their church in New Orleans where 90% of its members lost their homes. Two members perished during the storm. At times, with emotions still raw 20 years later, she shared stories of the difficulties, but also stories of community, of 100,000 volunteers coming from around the world to do whatever they could to care for those facing such devastation. She said, despite the darkness, they felt God‚Äôs light, hope and strength as they worked to rebuild.

As we listened to this story, and as we look to our future, it somehow made the challenges that St. Paul‚Äôs faces seem less daunting. We believe that God wanted us to hear these stories of faith, hope and resilience, and that in the darkest of trials, God was there.

What Stewardship Truly Means

We were reminded that Stewardship is not simply about money. It is how we take care of the resources that God has given us — our time, our financial gifts, our building, as well as our spiritual gifts and passions. All these come from God and are to be shared for the common good.

God wants us to be generous. Often when we reach out and bless others, we are greatly blessed in return.

Encouragement & Biblical Stories

We have read many stories in the Bible where God has done amazing things with people who gave what they could. We encourage you to reflect on the miraculous stories of The Widow‚Äôs Olive Oil (2 Kings 4:1-7) and Feeding the 5,000 (John 6:1-14).

A Call to Generosity & Hope

Generosity is a very personal thing, and we believe this comes down to our faith. When we truly believe God can do more than we can ever ask or imagine, the possibilities of what we can do together are amazing to think about.

We live in a hurting world that is aching for a message of hope. Perhaps God wants St. Paul‚Äôs to be an answer to prayers in our community, if we have the courage to step out in faith, and be generous with our time, talents and gifts - gifts that God has given each one of us.
