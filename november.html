What is Stewardship?

When we talk about stewardship, we want everyone to share a common understanding of how this ministry nurtures our life together as a church family.

As we gathered as a new team, we reached out to our National Church, as they have a department that focuses on stewardship. We spoke with Karen Plater, Associate Secretary of Stewardship at The Presbyterian Church in Canada. After our conversation, Karen asked if we would consider joining her in attending the annual Stewardship Conference offered by the Presbyterian Church (USA). She said this was an excellent conference and she liked to take a group of people from congregations across Canada to attend each year. She offered to pay our expenses to participate — an opportunity which felt like a God-given gift.

Stories from the Stewardship Conference

This year, the conference was held in New Orleans, and it became clear why this location was chosen - it was exactly 20 years ago that Hurricane Katrina devastated the city.

During the week, we heard stories of churches facing challenges similar to those St. Paul‚Äôs is working through today. Some were large congregations, while others quite small. Yet, we were inspired by stories of rebirth and renewal, and the realization that God was with them through it all.

New Orleans ¬∑ August 29, 2005 Hurricane Katrina left more than 80% of the city underwater, displacing families, shattering neighbourhoods, and calling churches to become anchors of relief and hope.

One pastor shared her story of leading her church in New Orleans through the storm. Ninety percent of their members lost their homes, and two members perished. With emotions still raw, she spoke about the difficulties, but also of community, where 100,000 volunteers came from around the globe to care for those facing such devastation. Despite the darkness, they felt God‚Äôs light, hope and strength, as they worked to rebuild.

As we listened to this story, and as we look to the future, it somehow made the challenges that St. Paul‚Äôs faces seem less daunting. We believe that God wanted us to hear these stories of faith, hope and resilience - a reminder that even in the darkest of trials, God is with us.

We also heard messages of generosity.

What Stewardship Truly Means

We were reminded that Stewardship is not simply about money. It is how we take care of the resources that God has given us — our time, our financial gifts, our building, as well as our spiritual gifts and passions. All these come from God and are to be shared for the common good.

God wants us to be generous. Often when we reach out and bless others, we are greatly blessed in return.
